Banana Pi M2 - first attempt

Posted on czw 21 stycznia 2016 in Embedded • 1 min read

Recently, I tried to make up and running brand new BananaPi M2.

My goal was to make dedicated camera (using CSI interface) work. TLDR: I have failed.

It looks like it is very important to distinct M2 version from BananaPi M1, plus or Pro. It seems that M2 is made by different company and for sure, it is not compatibile with older verisons. This was especially deceptive for me, because I have lost a lot of time until I finally figured it out.

It seems that there is no official Linux distribution, which will support all hardware on the board. I found some sane solutions like Armbian or Bananian, but they are still not considered as stable (at least by autors).

Installation of both is quite straight-forward: download, unzip, dd image on microSD card, and run. Simple, no magic here.

Links:

Armbian

Bananian

Bad news is that I haven't found a way to make camera work. From what I read, there is no hope, because support from manufactuer is bad or non-existent. Additionally, OV chip used in this camera is not supported by mainline Linux kernels.

All in all, I switched to ususal webcam, connected over USB.
